End of 4th Year

Graduation Picture

Yes, I have completed my 4th year at KAIST. However, I still have not taken the required number of credits(130) to graduate KAIST; therefore, I am still not a KAIST graduate. I will be just considered yeoncha cho-ga-jwa (연차초과자) because I went over the time limit that KAIST covers the tuition. My friends and I took the graduation photos beginning of June, it was so hot that wearing those suits almost killed us. I have enjoyed my times at KAIST with these guys. To introduce them from left to right: KyungJe Jo, Mr.Idon'tknowwho, Jason Cho, HyunSung Cho, Tony Kim, Me!, and lastly, Sunmin Son. I was able to survive the four grueling years at KAIST because their help. I was totally new to the computer science field; yet, with their help, I was able to become who I am now.

Final Exams

Of course, it's the final's exam week (our final week is basically two weeks since we have one week is for humanity courses and another week is for major exams). Speding time to study daily is almost coming to an end. Thankfully, I only had one final exam to take this semester. Although the result of the exam was terrible, I am still satisfied that I don't have to take anymore official exams for college courses. I just have to finish required research and collaborative project course to take next semester, which is going to be one year from now on. Yes, I will tell you why it's a year from now next.

Future Plans

I have decided to take a semester off from school. This means that I will have time from now... till beginning of March of 2018. The reason I have decided to take a semester off is pretty straight forward. I needed a break; I was burned out from due dates and assignments. I wanted to do my own studies that I am interested in. It's not that all of the things I studied were topics that I didn't want to learn at school. For now, I am planning on training myself to become a full stack developer. Why full stack developer? I am interested in human computer interaction field (I am not sure which specific topic yet..) and to study HCI (or any other field, I assume) effectively, I realized that being able to code up a prototype was essential. Therefore, I decided to cover both frontend and backend. This semester truly taught me a lot of life and work lessons. I thank my research advisor Juho Kim for an invaluable experience.
